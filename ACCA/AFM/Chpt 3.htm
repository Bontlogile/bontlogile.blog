Financial Analysis

Ratio analysis is important to different stakeholders because it provides clear and concise insights into a company's financial health, performance, and risks, enabling informed decisions tailored to each stakeholder's needs:

    Investors

  • Helps evaluate profitability, growth potential, dividend safety, and stock valuation to decide whether to buy, hold, or sell shares. It reveals how well the company uses resources to generate returns.

    • Creditors and Lenders

  • Assesses the company’s ability to repay loans by analyzing liquidity, solvency, and leverage. This informs lending decisions and risk assessment, ensuring creditors minimize default risk.

    • Management

  • Measures operational efficiency, cost control, and financial stability. It supports strategic planning, resource allocation, budgeting, and performance monitoring to improve company performance.

    • Analysts and Advisors

  • Enables benchmarking against peers, detecting trends, and identifying competitive advantages or weaknesses. This supports recommendations for investment or business improvement.

    • Regulators and Authorities

  • Provides a tool for monitoring compliance, financial soundness, and early detection of distress or fraudulent activity for protection of stakeholders and market integrity.

    • Employees and Trade Unions

  • Offers insight into company stability and profitability, which affects job security, wage negotiations, and benefits.

    • Financial ratio analysis simplifies complex financial data into understandable metrics, helping stakeholders assess a company’s financial condition and make better informed, data-driven decisions aligned with their interests and roles.
